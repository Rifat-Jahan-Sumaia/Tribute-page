Dr. Muhammad Yunus

A Visionary for Social Change


Timestamps of Dr. Yunus' Life:

  • 1940: Born on June 28 in Chittagong, Bangladesh.
  • 1965: Earns a Fulbright scholarship to study Economics at Vanderbilt University, USA.
  • 1969: Receives a PhD in Economics from Vanderbilt University.
  • 1972: Returns to Bangladesh after the country's independence and becomes a professor at Chittagong University.
  • 1976: Begins experimenting with microloans in Jobra village, laying the groundwork for the Grameen Bank.
  • 1983: Establishes the Grameen Bank to provide microloans to the poor without collateral.
  • 2006: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his work in microfinance and social business.
  • 2008: Launches the Yunus Centre, a global hub for social business.
  • 2010: Advocates for the concept of social business and publishes Building Social Business.
  • 2020: Continues to promote poverty alleviation and social business worldwide through speaking engagements and initiatives.


Key Achievements:

  • Independence Award (1987): The highest civilian award in Bangladesh for his outstanding contribution to social development.
  • Nobel Peace Prize (2006): Awarded to Yunus and the Grameen Bank for their efforts to create economic and social development through microcredit.
  • Medal of Freedom (2009): One of the highest civilian awards in the United States, recognizing his contributions to social business and poverty alleviation.
  • Congressional Gold Medal (2010): Awarded in recognition of his humanitarian work and commitment to social business.
